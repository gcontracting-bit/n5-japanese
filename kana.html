Special Rules

Long Vowels

Hiragana: Written by adding the vowel character. Example: おかあさん (okaasan - mother)

Katakana: Use the prolongation mark ー. Example: コーヒー (koohii - coffee)

Double Consonants (っ/ッ)

Small つ (hiragana) or ツ (katakana) doubles the following consonant, creating a pause.

Examples: きっぷ (kippu - ticket), マッチ (macchi - match)

Small ya/yu/yo

Written half-size (ゃゅょ in hiragana, ャュョ in katakana) to form combination sounds.

Example: きょう (kyou - today)
