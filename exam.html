▶Question Types

Vocabulary Section

  • Kanji reading: Choose the correct hiragana reading for underlined kanji.
  • Orthography: Choose the correct kanji/katakana for an underlined hiragana word.
  • Contextual meaning: Choose the word that fits the blank in a sentence.
  • Paraphrasing: Choose the sentence closest in meaning to the given sentence.

Grammar Section

  • Fill the blank: Choose the correct grammar form for the blank.
  • Sentence assembly: Rearrange four scrambled parts into the correct order (identify what goes in position ★).
  • Passage grammar: Choose correct grammar within a short text passage.

Reading Section

  • Short passages: Read 2–3 short texts and answer comprehension questions.
  • Information retrieval: Read a notice, schedule, or sign and answer questions about it.

Listening Section

  • Task-based: Listen to a conversation and choose the correct answer/picture.
  • Key point: Listen and identify the main point of the conversation.
  • Verbal response: Listen to a short statement and choose the appropriate reply.
Audio is played ONCE only. You cannot replay it. Practice listening without pausing or rewinding.

▶Test Day Strategy

Before the Exam

  • Get a full night of sleep the night before
  • Eat a proper meal beforehand
  • Bring your admission voucher
  • Bring photo ID
  • Bring HB / No. 2 pencils (no pens allowed)
  • Bring an eraser
  • Bring a watch — not all test rooms have visible clocks

Time Management

  • Vocabulary + Grammar (75 min total): Spend no more than 30–40 seconds on any single vocabulary or grammar question. If unsure, mark your best guess and move on.
  • Save 15–20 min for reading passages — they take longer per question and are worth the time investment.
  • Answer every question — there is no penalty for guessing. Eliminate choices you know are wrong, then pick from the rest.

Section-Specific Tips

  • Reading strategy: Read the question first, then scan the passage for the answer. You don't need to understand every word — look for keywords. For information retrieval (schedules, signs), focus on numbers, dates, and key nouns.
  • Listening strategy: Read the answer choices during the short pause before audio plays. This tells you what to listen for. Don't panic if you miss a word — focus on overall meaning. The correct answer usually contains a keyword from the audio.

Kanji reading, word meaning, word usage. Quick pace — 30–40 seconds per question max.

Grammar fill-in, sentence assembly, and passage comprehension. Save 15–20 min for reading.

Short break between written and listening sections. Stretch, hydrate, stay calm.

Audio played once. Read choices during pauses. Focus on keywords and overall meaning.
