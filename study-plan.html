12-Week Study Plan

JLPT N5 — Target: Sunday 5 July 2026

on-track

Daily Routine Templates

Weekday (45-60 min)
  • 5-10 min: Anki / flashcard review
  • 20-30 min: Genki chapter work (or kanji from week 3+)
  • 15 min: Listening practice (from week 6+)
Weekend (1.5-2 hours)
  • 10-15 min: Anki review
  • 30-40 min: Genki + full exercises
  • 15-20 min: Reading (NHK Easy / simple passages)
  • 15-20 min: Listening practice
  • 30 min: Practice tests (from week 9+)

▶ Week 1: Hiragana Lockdown + Genki Ch 1 (Apr 13 – Apr 19)

Focus: Master all remaining hiragana (combos, handakuten) and complete Genki I Chapter 1.

  • Drill the 49 hiragana still in "studying" — ya/ra/wa rows, ん, handakuten (pa row), all combo sounds (きゃ, しゃ, ちゃ, etc.)
  • Clear up the 5 amber characters: ぬ, ほ, ぜ, ぢ, づ
  • Complete Genki I Ch 1: greetings, self-introductions, は particle, です/じゃないです
  • Start Anki deck — add 8-10 new words per day from Genki vocab lists
Checkpoint: Read any hiragana character on sight. ~60 vocab words. Can introduce yourself in Japanese.

▶ Week 2: Katakana Blitz + Genki Ch 2 (Apr 20 – Apr 26)

Focus: Learn all katakana (basic 46 + dakuten + combos). Complete Genki I Chapter 2.

  • Katakana basic rows (ア–ン) — use same drill method that worked for hiragana
  • Katakana dakuten + handakuten + combos
  • Genki I Ch 2: これ/それ/あれ, の particle, も particle, numbers, prices
  • Practice reading katakana loan words (コーヒー, テレビ, パソコン, etc.)
  • Continue Anki: 8-10 new words/day
Checkpoint: Read both kana scripts fluently. ~120 vocab words. Can ask/answer basic questions about things and prices.

▶ Week 3: First Kanji + Genki Ch 3 (Apr 27 – May 3)

Focus: Begin kanji study (first ~15 kanji). Complete Genki I Chapter 3.

  • Start N5 kanji: 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十 百 千 万 円 日
  • Genki I Ch 3: verb types (u/ru/irregular), を particle, で particle, に (destination), frequency adverbs
  • Practice writing basic sentences with particles: [place]で[activity]をします
  • Anki: continue 8-10 new words/day + add kanji cards
Checkpoint: ~180 vocab. Recognise 15 kanji. Can describe daily activities using verbs and particles.

▶ Week 4: Verb Conjugation + Genki Ch 4 (May 4 – May 10)

Focus: Verb conjugation (past tense, negatives). Genki I Chapter 4. Kanji batch 2.

  • Genki I Ch 4: past tense (ました/ませんでした), time expressions, も with verbs
  • Drill verb conjugation patterns — present/past, positive/negative
  • Kanji batch 2 (~15 more): 月 火 水 木 金 土 年 時 間 分 半 今 何 人 本
  • Anki: 10 new words/day — you need to start pushing harder here
Checkpoint: ~250 vocab, ~30 kanji. Can talk about past events. Conjugate verbs in 4 forms.

▶ Week 5: Adjectives + Counters + Genki Ch 5 (May 11 – May 17)

Focus: い/な adjectives, counters. Genki I Chapter 5. Kanji batch 3.

  • Genki I Ch 5: い-adjectives and な-adjectives (present and past), すき/きらい, counters (つ, 人, 枚)
  • Drill adjective conjugation: おおきい→おおきくない→おおきかった→おおきくなかった
  • Kanji batch 3 (~15 more): 大 小 中 上 下 山 川 田 学 校 先 生 私 友 母
  • Weekend: review all Genki Ch 1-5 grammar points — catch anything slipping
Checkpoint: ~320 vocab, ~45 kanji. Can describe people, places, and preferences using adjectives.

▶ Week 6: Te-form + Listening Kickoff + Genki Ch 6 (May 18 – May 24)

Focus: Te-form (the big one). Start daily listening practice. Genki I Chapter 6.

  • Genki I Ch 6: て-form, ている (ongoing actions), requests (てください)
  • Memorise te-form rules — this is critical, drill until automatic
  • Start 15 min daily listening: Genki audio, JapanesePod101 beginner, or Nihongo con Teppei
  • Kanji batch 4 (~13 more): 父 子 女 男 目 口 手 足 車 電 話 食 飲
Checkpoint: ~400 vocab, ~58 kanji. Can make requests, describe ongoing actions. Te-form is second nature.

▶ Week 7: Short Forms + Genki Ch 7-8 (May 25 – May 31)

Focus: Short/plain forms, casual speech. Push through two Genki chapters this week.

  • Genki I Ch 7: short forms (dictionary, ない, past), と思う, と言う, casual speech
  • Genki I Ch 8: short form + noun clauses, てあげる/もらう/くれる, comparisons
  • This is a heavy week — use weekday sessions for Ch 7, weekend for Ch 8
  • Kanji batch 5 (~15 more): 読 書 見 聞 言 行 来 出 入 休 買 新 古 長 高
  • Continue listening practice (15 min/day)
Checkpoint: ~480 vocab, ~73 kanji. Can use casual and polite forms. Understand giving/receiving.

▶ Week 8: Want/Desire + Genki Ch 9-10 (Jun 1 – Jun 7)

Focus: Another double-chapter week. たい form, ことがある, と conditional.

  • Genki I Ch 9: past experience (ことがある), comparison with より, superlatives (一番)
  • Genki I Ch 10: たい (want to), たり…たりする, ～ことができる (ability)
  • Kanji push — finish remaining N5 kanji (~30 more): 安 白 百 千 万 外 国 名 前 後 午 毎 週 道 駅 etc.
  • Weekend: read a short NHK Easy article start to finish
Checkpoint: ~570 vocab, ~103 kanji (all N5 kanji!). Can express desires, experiences, abilities.

▶ Week 9: Genki Ch 11-12 + First Practice Test (Jun 8 – Jun 14)

Focus: Final Genki chapters for N5 scope. First full practice test.

  • Genki I Ch 11: ～たいと思っている, ～てほしい, ～ないでください
  • Genki I Ch 12: ～んです, ～すぎる, ～ほうがいい, ～ので/から
  • Vocab blitz: 15 new words/day — you need ~700 by end of this week
  • Weekend: take your FIRST full N5 practice test (timed). Score it honestly.
Checkpoint: ~700 vocab, all 103 kanji solid. Practice test taken — identify your weak sections (vocab/grammar/reading/listening).

▶ Week 10: Weak-Spot Assault (Jun 15 – Jun 21)

Focus: Attack whatever the practice test exposed. Fill vocab gaps.

  • Review practice test results — rank your weakest areas and focus sessions there
  • Grammar weak? Re-drill the Genki exercises for those chapters
  • Listening weak? Double listening time to 30 min/day
  • Reading weak? One NHK Easy article per day, look up every word you don't know
  • Vocab push: 12-15 new words/day, heavy Anki reviews
  • Weekend: second practice test — compare score to week 9
Checkpoint: ~780 vocab. Practice test #2 should show clear improvement over #1. Gap areas narrowing.

▶ Week 11: Exam Simulation Mode (Jun 22 – Jun 28)

Focus: Simulate real exam conditions. Build stamina and timing.

  • Timed section drills — practise each JLPT section under time pressure
  • Vocab/kanji: maintenance only (Anki reviews, no new cards)
  • Grammar review: one quick pass through all Genki grammar points
  • Listen to full-length N5 listening sections back-to-back
  • Weekend: full timed practice exam #3 under strict conditions (no phone, no pausing)
Checkpoint: 800+ vocab. Scoring 70%+ on practice tests. Comfortable with exam timing.

▶ Week 12: Final Week — Stay Sharp, Don't Cram (Jun 29 – Jul 5)

Focus: Light review. Confidence building. Rest before exam day.

  • Mon-Wed: light Anki reviews + one listening session per day. No new material.
  • Thu: quick scan of grammar cheat sheet — refresh any tricky points
  • Fri: one final practice test if you want, or just relax
  • Sat (Jul 4): rest. Prepare your exam materials (pencils, eraser, watch, photo ID). Get to bed early.
  • Sun (Jul 5): EXAM DAY. Trust your preparation. You've put in the work.
Checkpoint: You're ready. 800+ vocab, 103 kanji, all Genki I grammar, dozens of practice hours. Go get it.
